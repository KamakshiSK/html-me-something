India

  • Delhi

    • Delhi is famous for its tourist attractions, food and it's marketplaces. Since its the national capital of the country I enjoyed exploring rashtrapati bhavan, parliament house, India gate. I have also visited historic places like qutub minar, humayun's tomb.

  • Mumbai

    • Mumbai is the financial, commercial and entertainment capital of India. I love Mumbai street food like Pav Bhaji, Wada Pav.

  • Goa

    • Goa is my favorite tourist destination in India. It is famous for its beaches, sizzling nightlife, hippie markets, and the glitz & glamor. Goa has influence of Portuguese rule and Latin culture. Goan sea food is mouth watering.

  • Amritsar

    • Golden Temple in Amritsar is a peaceful destination. Lunger served in temple is the most fulfilling and satisfying meal I ever had. Other must try food in Amritsar is sweet buttermilk, paranthas, butter chicken.

  • Ladakh

    • Ladakh is land of high passes. Me and my husband along with other 50 bikers rode to Khardung-la which is one of the claimed hightest motorable roads in the World. Nature is at best with the views of mountains, rivers, blue sky with clouds you feel you can almost touch.

Europe

Europe is one of the smallest continents in the world but I think it the the most beautiful one with staggering alpine views, fields of lavender and vine, limestone cliffs. Most of my six months in Europe I was in Belgium but totally envy beautiful Europe.

  • Belgium

    • Belgium is famous for waffles, fries, chocolate and beer. Some of the famous landmarks I visited are Notre Dame du Sablon church, Miniature garden (Mini Europe), shopping mall Galeries Royales Saint-Hubert, Manneken Pis, Atomium and Grand Palace.

  • Paris

    • What would Paris be without its symbolic Eiffel Tower? The magnificent view of Paris from Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame Cathedral, Louvre Museum, Arc of Triumph, Palace of Versailles, Luxembourg park and Disneyland Paris are highlights of Paris visit.

  • Amterdam

    • Keukenhof garden near Amsterdam is my favorite. I never thought there exits so many varieties of beautiful tulips.

China

China is a fascinating and rapidly changing country. Old customs and habits hang on as modern skyscrapers go up every second, the country becomes more of a global powerhouse, and people from around the world move there.

  • Beijing

    • Badaling Pass and the Great Wall of China, Summer palace, Fragrant Hills Park, The Ming Tombs, Tiananmen square are some of the high of Beijing.

  • Shanghai

    • Shanghai, on China’s central coast, is the country's biggest city and a global financial hub. Its heart is the Bund, a famed waterfront promenade lined with colonial-era buildings. Across the Huangpu River rises the Pudong district’s futuristic skyline, including 632m Shanghai Tower and the Oriental Pearl TV Tower, with distinctive pink spheres. Sprawling Yu Garden has traditional pavilions, towers and ponds.

USA

  • Wisconsin

    • Many people refer to Wisconsin as the Cheese State or the Dairy State, but there is more to the area than Dairy production. Wisconsin is home to many desirable travel locations from big cities to national parks, offering a range of sights and attractions for thrill seekers, nature lovers and families. Travelers can find amusement and relaxation year-round in Wisconsin though tourists should be prepared for any type of weather if they want to have the best possible vacation.Door county, Washingtion Island, Harley Davidson Museum in Milwaukee, Wisconsin Dells

  • California

    • California beckons visitors with sunshine, stunning landscapes, and urban sophistication. A place of dreams, this alluring state has it all - spectacular sandy beaches, snow-capped mountains, idyllic farmlands, and ancient redwood groves, along with interesting, multicultural cities. Another top reason to visit California is the great outdoors.

    • Los Angeles

      • Los Angeles epitomizes the sunny Southern California lifestyle, with its wide, palm-fringed boulevards; exclusive designer boutiques; and sun-drenched beaches. Rodeo Drive, Beverley Hills, Santa Monica, Veniece Beach, Walk of Fame on Hollywood Boulevard, Hollywood sign are famous tourist destination. And not to miss the Universal Studios, Disneyland California are my favorite theme parks.

    • San Fransico

      • This famous city is known for its eclectic culture and surreal beauty. When the afternoon sun shines on the steep hillsides or the fog rolls in from the Pacific Ocean over the Golden Gate Bridge, the city has a mystical quality. Goldengate bridge, fisherman's Wharf, China town, Lombart Street are fun places to visit.

    • San Diego

      • Blessed with a balmy year-round climate and beautiful beaches, San Diego is the quintessential Southern California city. The main attractions are Balboa Park, La Jolla beach, San Diego Zoo and Sea World.

    • Yosemite National Park

      • A masterpiece created by Mother Nature, the Yosemite Valley is surrounded by sheer granite cliffs that were carved by glaciers millions of years ago. The highlights of Yosemite are Yosemite Falls, a dramatic waterfall more than 2,000 feet tall, and Half Dome, an iconic sheer-faced granite mountain. Yet every area of the park is gorgeous, from its expansive meadows and crystal-clear streams to the giant sequoia tree groves.

    • Central Coast and Big Sur

      • With its wooded hillsides, undulating farmlands, and endless stretches of sandy beaches, the Central Coast is an inviting part of California to explore.

    • Santa Barbara

      • One of the loveliest seaside cities in California, this resort destination is a beach lover's paradise, with balmy weather and an attractive coastline of golden sandy beaches. Santa Barbara is also a historic town steeped in old-world charm. The 18th-century Mission Santa Barbara is known as the "Queen of the Missions" and is surrounded by flowering gardens, the Santa Barbara Courthouse exemplifies exquisite Spanish Moorish style, and most of downtown Santa Barbara is graced by traditional Spanish-style architecture.

    • Mammoth Lakes

      • Mammoth Lakes draws many visitors for outdoor adventures and boasts about 300 sunny days a year. The crisp mountain air invigorates the body and soul, while the beautiful scenery provides an amazing setting for hiking and mountain biking. Mammoth Lakes has more than 300 miles of hiking trails in diverse wilderness areas such as Devils Postpile National Monument and the Pacific Crest Trail. The landscape varies from peaceful meadows and pine forests to granite crags, including a summit that soars to 11,053 feet. With nearly a hundred lakes, many stocked with trout, Mammoth Lakes is an incredible place for fishing during summertime.

  • Nevada

    • Fabulous Las Vegas, Hoover dam, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area, Great Basin National Park are highlights of the visit.

  • Arizona

    • Grand Canyon National Park, in Arizona, is home to much of the immense Grand Canyon, with its layered bands of red rock revealing millions of years of geological history. Antelope Canyon is a slot canyon in the American Southwest. It is on Navajo land east of Page, Arizona. Antelope Canyon includes two separate, scenic slot canyon sections, referred to individually as "Upper Antelope Canyon" or "The Crack"; and "Lower Antelope Canyon" or "The Corkscrew".

  • Texas

    • They say that everything is bigger in Texas, and it is certainly true that there is a large range of things do so, see and explore in the second largest state. Texans are fiercely proud, and they have a lot to be proud of. Whether you’re interested in history, art or cowboy culture, it’s all found in Texas. Dallas, Austin, NASA Huston, San Antonio, Galveston beach are some of the places I have visited in Texas.

  • Florida

    • With more than 8,000 miles of shoreline, the Sunshine State is prime vacation territory. But with so many diverse destinations in Florida, it can be hard to pick which place is right for your next getaway. Of all the Disneylands I have visited, the one in Florida is my favorite.

  • Missouri

    • “The Show Me State” has lots to offer visitors, from impressive natural scenery and outdoor adventures, to exciting cities with interesting museums and lots more. So far I have visited St. Louis Gateway Arch, Branson, Forest Park, Lake of the Ozarks, Jefferson City.
